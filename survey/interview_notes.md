# Practitioner note (qualitative demand evidence)

A first-hand account from the author, who actively works as an independent
high-school tutor. It supports the value proposition, the willingness-to-pay
argument, and the market wedge. (A formal survey instrument and interview guide
for a wider field round are in `README.md` / `results_sample.csv`.)

## Author — practicing high-school tutor

**What the workflow is today:** After each student's mock exam I go through the
answer sheet by hand, find the topics they keep missing, and write the parent a
short update on strengths/weaknesses and what to practise next. For a full mock
this is roughly an hour per student, and it is the part of the job that is pure
routine — exactly what ClassLens automates.

**Why families come to tutoring:** The students/families who leave big cram
schools generally do so because the class is one-size-fits-all — parents cannot
see, exam by exam, where their child is actually weak. Personalised feedback is
the thing they are paying a tutor for.

**What this supports:** the value proposition (automate the routine diagnosis and
parent report; keep the teaching with the teacher), the wedge (large cram schools
under-serve customisation, so demand shifts to per-student diagnosis), and the
go-to-market channel (the author and peer tutors are reachable first users).

**Honest limitation:** this is single-practitioner evidence, not market proof; the
survey instrument exists to validate it more widely.
